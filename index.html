Automatic Street Light Controller using Op-Amp Comparator

This project demonstrates a real-life application of Operational Amplifier (Op-Amp) using comparator configuration to automatically control street lights based on surrounding light intensity.

About the Project

What is Op-Amp?

An Operational Amplifier is a high-gain electronic device used in amplification, comparison and signal processing applications.

Comparator Application

In this project, the Op-Amp works as a comparator that compares sensor voltage with reference voltage.

Objective

To automatically switch ON street lights during darkness and switch OFF during daytime.

Working Principle

🌞 Day Time Operation

During daytime, sufficient light falls on the LDR (Light Dependent Resistor). Due to high light intensity, the resistance of the LDR decreases significantly. This causes the voltage at the input terminal of the Op-Amp comparator to become lower than the reference voltage. The Operational Amplifier compares the sensor voltage with the reference voltage and generates a LOW output condition. As a result, current does not flow through the LED (Street Light), and the street light remains switched OFF. This automatic operation helps in preventing unnecessary power consumption during daytime and improves energy efficiency.

🌙 Night Time Operation

During nighttime or low-light conditions, the intensity of light falling on the LDR decreases. As a result, the resistance of the LDR increases automatically. This increases the voltage at the comparator input terminal. When the sensor voltage becomes greater than the reference voltage, the Op-Amp comparator generates a HIGH output. The HIGH output allows current to pass through the LED (Street Light), which automatically turns ON the street light system. Thus, the circuit intelligently controls the street light automatically based on surrounding environmental light conditions.

Automatic Working Flow

💡 Automatic Street Light Control

The LDR continuously senses surrounding light intensity and converts it into an electrical signal. The Op-Amp comparator compares this signal with a predefined reference voltage. Based on the comparison result, the circuit automatically controls the street light output. This system eliminates manual operation and provides intelligent automatic switching of street lights.

Project Diagram & Working

Automatic Street Light Controller using Op-Amp Comparator

The above diagram represents the complete working model of an Automatic Street Light Controller using an Operational Amplifier (Op-Amp) configured as a comparator. The system uses an LDR (Light Dependent Resistor) to detect surrounding light intensity. During daytime, the intensity of light falling on the LDR is high, which decreases its resistance. This changes the voltage at the comparator input terminal, causing the Op-Amp output to remain LOW. As a result, the LED street light remains switched OFF automatically. During nighttime, the surrounding light intensity decreases and the resistance of the LDR increases automatically. This causes the comparator input voltage to rise above the reference voltage. The Op-Amp comparator then generates a HIGH output signal which turns ON the LED street light automatically. The Op-Amp acts as a comparator that continuously compares the sensor voltage with a fixed reference voltage. Depending on the comparison result, the output changes between HIGH and LOW states. This automatic switching mechanism helps in reducing electricity wastage and improves energy efficiency. The circuit also contains resistors for voltage division and current limiting purposes. The 220Ω resistor protects the LED from excessive current flow, while the voltage divider network stabilizes the input signal provided to the comparator circuit. This project demonstrates a practical and energy-efficient real-life application of Operational Amplifier circuits in automation systems, smart lighting systems, parking area lighting, garden lighting, and smart city infrastructure. The automatic operation eliminates the need for manual switching of street lights and provides a cost-effective solution for energy conservation.
